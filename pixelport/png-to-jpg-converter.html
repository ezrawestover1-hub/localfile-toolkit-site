Convert PNG Images to JPG Without Uploading Them

Use a local PNG to JPG converter when a photo workflow or destination needs an opaque JPEG. PixelRefinery opens the PNG in your browser, lets you choose a background for transparent pixels, and creates the JPG locally.

Important: JPG cannot preserve alpha transparency. Use a white or black matte for transparent artwork, and expect a lossy output format.

When PNG to JPG is useful

PNG is strong for transparency and crisp graphics, while JPG is commonly used for photographs and broadly compatible image delivery. A PNG screenshot or logo may become a JPG for a form that accepts JPEG only, but a transparent logo can develop an unwanted edge if the background is not chosen deliberately.

How to convert a PNG to JPG

  1. Open the PixelRefinery image converter and select one PNG under 20 MB.
  2. Check the source preview and dimensions shown in the browser.
  3. Choose JPG, set the width or height if needed, and select white or black for transparent pixels.
  4. Set an appropriate quality value, then choose Convert and download.

What changes during conversion

JPG is lossy, so repeated exports or a low quality setting can introduce blocking, ringing, or softened edges. PNG metadata, color-management details, animation, and every source-specific feature are not promised to survive a canvas conversion. Review the downloaded image in its destination workflow.

PNG to JPG questions

Can I make a transparent PNG have a white JPG background?

Yes. Load the PNG, choose JPG, and select Fill white in the background control before downloading.

Will the JPG be smaller than the PNG?

It often can be for photographic content, but file size depends on dimensions, image detail, and quality. PixelRefinery does not promise a specific reduction.

Does PixelRefinery send my PNG to a server?

No. The image is decoded and converted in the browser, and the converter is shipped with a policy that blocks background network connections.
